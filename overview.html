Project Explore

Where big adventures spark bright futures!

An After-School Journey for Grades K-4

Project Explore is a unique after-school program teaching entrepreneurship, financial literacy, and social-emotional learning (SEL). Through a captivating animated series and hands-on activities, we transform complex concepts into fun, memorable adventures!

Our Unique Approach

Picture Book Storytelling

Each module begins with a beautifully illustrated story featuring Alex, Mia, Taylor, and Benny the Bear, introducing core concepts in a fun way.

Hands-On Activities

Learning is solidified through creative, hands-on projects. From building a lemonade stand to designing a piggy bank, students apply what they've learned.

Our Core Pillars

Entrepreneurship

Students learn business basics, creative problem-solving, and bringing ideas to life through fun, hands-on projects.

Financial Literacy

We introduce essential money concepts like saving and spending, building a strong foundation for financial well-being.

Social-Emotional Learning

Our curriculum emphasizes teamwork, collaboration, and emotional expression, helping students develop crucial life skills.

A Glimpse into Our Adventures

1

The Lemonade Stand

The Story

Alex, Mia, and friends decide to start a lemonade stand! With help from their neighbor Mr. Johnson, they learn about counting, pricing, and serving customers in a fun business adventure.

The Activity

Students get creative by designing and building their very own paper lemonade stand, complete with colorful signs, pretend lemons, and cups.

2

Money and Choices

The Story

The friends visit the Magical Toy Market! They learn valuable lessons about the difference between needs and wants, making smart choices, and the importance of saving money.

The Activity

To practice saving, each student designs and decorates their own personalized DIY piggy bank using recycled materials, paint, and their imagination.

3

Teamwork and Collaboration

The Story

An exciting quest into the Enchanted Forest teaches the friends that they must work together, listen to each other's ideas, and combine their strengths to find hidden treasures.

The Activity

In small groups, students practice collaboration by creating a large-scale team mural or collage that represents their shared ideas and teamwork.
